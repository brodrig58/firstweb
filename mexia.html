Ynex Mexia

Born: May 24th, 1870
Primarily worked for: California Academy of Sciences
University: California, Berkeley

Biography

Ynes Mexia was a trailblazing Mexican-American botanist who defied the odds to make lasting contributions to the study of plants, particularly in Mexico and South America. She didn’t begin her botanical career until her 50s, but once she did, she threw herself into fieldwork, enduring tough conditions in remote jungles and mountains. Over the course of her expeditions, Mexia discovered more than 500 new plant species, earning the respect of leading scientific institutions like the Smithsonian. Despite the challenges of being a woman in a male-dominated field, Mexia’s determination and passion for botany shone through, and her legacy lives on in the many species named after her and in the inspiration she continues to provide for future generations of scientists. .

Notable Contributions:

  • Discovered over 500 new plant species.
  • Traveled to remove areas in Latin America, collecting plant specimens that helped expand botanical knowledge.
  • Her plant collections are kept at the U.S. National Herbarium.

Famous Quote:

"I was not a botanist when I started, I began this work because I loved nature."
